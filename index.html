FEATURES OF GLOBAL WARMING

Global warming refers to the long-term increase in Earth's average surface temperature due to human activities, primarily the burning of fossil fuels. Here are some key features of global warming:

1. Rising Temperatures

Earth's average temperature has increased significantly over the past century. Heatwaves have become more frequent and intense.

2. Melting Ice Caps and Glaciers

Polar ice caps in Antarctica and the Arctic are melting at an alarming rate. Glaciers worldwide are shrinking, leading to rising sea levels.

3. Rising Sea Levels

Due to melting ice and the thermal expansion of seawater, sea levels are increasing. Coastal regions face an increased risk of flooding and land loss.

4. Extreme Weather Events

There is an increased occurrence of hurricanes, typhoons, and cyclones. More frequent droughts, heatwaves, and heavy rainfall events are also being observed.

6. Disruptions in Ecosystems and Biodiversity Loss

Many species are struggling to adapt to changing climates. Forests, wetlands, and marine habitats are being destroyed.

7. Agriculture and Food Security Risks

Crop yields are declining due to rising temperatures and unpredictable rainfall. There is also an increase in pests and diseases affecting crops and livestock.

8. Increased Frequency of Wildfires

Hotter temperatures and drier conditions contribute to more frequent wildfires, resulting in loss of biodiversity and property damage.

9. Human Health Impacts

There are more cases of heat-related illnesses and deaths. Changing climate patterns also contribute to the spread of diseases.

10. Socioeconomic and Political Challenges

Climate refugees are increasing due to rising sea levels and extreme weather events. Economic losses are being observed in industries like agriculture, fisheries, and tourism.
